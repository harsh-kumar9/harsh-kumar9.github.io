I am a third-year PhD student in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Toronto, working under the supervision of Joseph Jay Williams. My research intersects cognition, mental health, and education, with a focus on utilizing technology, like Large Language Models (LLMs), to transform lives. I explore disciplines such as Human-Computer Interaction and Psychology to enhance well-being and education through technology. My work includes studies on how LLMs can promote mindfulness and the design of LLM-based educational tools that facilitate genuine learning. I use Reinforcement Learning algorithms, like contextual bandits, to create personalized systems that adapt and improve over time, emphasizing inclusivity and tailored experiences for each user. In the past, I interned at Microsoft Research in the Computational Social Science group working with Dan Goldstein, Jake Hofman, and David Rothschild.
